The process can be written in terms of carries `q_i = floor(a_i / 2)`,
where `a_i` is the number of stones present when position `i` is processed.
If `b_i` is the singleton indicator, then for `i >= 3`:

```text
2 q_i + b_i = q_{i-1} + q_{i-3}.
```

Having no singletons from position `k` onward is equivalent to
`q_{k-3} = q_{k-2} = q_{k-1}`.  Writing each earlier carry as this common
tail value plus an offset gives two linear boundary equations in the bits
`b_i`.  The loneliness condition is just the local rule that selected bit
positions must be at least three apart.

The solver derives the linear coefficients, splits the bit positions in two,
and uses meet-in-the-middle.  The right half is grouped by the boundary sum
and its first two bits; the left half probes matching right states and uses a
sorted tail-offset list to keep only nonnegative tail values.  The two sample
values are asserted before printing `S(80)`.
