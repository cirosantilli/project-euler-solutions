# Project Euler 513 Solution - Integral Median

<https://projecteuler.net/problem=513>:

* [513.py](513.py)

For sides `a <= b <= c`, the median to side `c` satisfies

\[
m^2=\frac{2a^2+2b^2-c^2}{4}.
\]

The fast solver uses a parameterization that turns the valid primitive triangles
into lattice points in a small collection of rational trapezoids.  The final
answer is then recovered from primitive counts by subtracting odd scaled copies.

## Trapezoid Counts

The core operation is a sum of floors over an interval:

\[
\sum_x \left\lfloor\frac{sx+t}{d}\right\rfloor.
\]

This counts lattice points below a rational line.  The helper
`trapezoid_floor_sum` applies the Euclidean floor-sum transformation: peel off
integer parts of the slope and intercept, swap the axes, and continue with a
smaller denominator.  Short intervals are evaluated directly.

The parity-restricted helper shifts the line and doubles the denominator so it
counts only lattice points in specified `x` and `y` residue classes modulo `2`.
That is the performance-critical replacement for scanning candidate parameters.

## Primitive Region

The primitive counter splits the parameter space into two ranges.  In both
ranges the admissible inequalities are linear-fractional, so they become a few
trapezoid floor-sum calls.  The loops over `s,t` and `u,v` only reach about
`sqrt(3n/2)`, and each inner region is counted in bulk.

## Odd Scaling Recurrence

The primitive count excludes triangles that are odd multiples of smaller
primitive configurations.  The full function satisfies an inclusion recurrence
over odd scale factors:

\[
F(n)=f(n)-\sum_{\substack{k\ge 3\\k\text{ odd}}}F(\lfloor n/k\rfloor).
\]

Equal quotients `floor(n/k)` are grouped, and the results are memoized.  The
solver checks `F(10)=3` and `F(50)=165`, then prints `F(100000)`.
