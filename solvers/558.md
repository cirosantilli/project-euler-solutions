# Project Euler 558 Solution - Irrational Base

<https://projecteuler.net/problem=558>:

* [558.py](558.py)

Let `r` be the real root of `x^3 - x^2 - 1 = 0`, so:

\[
r^3 = r^2 + 1.
\]

Multiplying by a power of `r` gives:

\[
r^{e+1}=r^e+r^{e-2}.
\]

This identity explains the shape of the greedy expansion. Once the largest
usable term `r^e` has been chosen, the remainder is smaller than `r^(e-2)`,
so the next exponent can be at most `e - 3`.

## Fixed-point power table

The solver avoids floating-point comparisons in the main loop. It first builds
a finite table of powers:

\[
\lfloor r^e \cdot 10^{90}\rfloor
\]

for a wide enough exponent interval. The powers are generated from the cubic
recurrence after computing `r` with high-precision decimal Newton iteration.

After that, every greedy comparison and subtraction is integer arithmetic.

## Greedy length

For an integer `n`, the solver:

1. finds the largest precomputed power not exceeding `n`;
2. subtracts it;
3. resumes the search three exponents lower;
4. repeats until no retained power fits.

The number of chosen powers is `l(n)`.

## Summing square lengths

For the target value:

\[
S(m)=\sum_{j=1}^{m} l(j^2),\qquad m=5{,}000{,}000,
\]

the squares are processed in increasing order. That means the pointer to the
leading power only moves forward through the table over the whole run.

## Checks

The implementation verifies:

- `l(3) = 4`
- `l(4) = 4`
- `l(10) = 3`
- `S(10) = 61`
- `S(1000) = 19403`
