Length `k` factors of the Fibonacci word are the lower mechanical words with
slope `1 / phi^2`.  If their breakpoints are ordered by
`{m / phi}`, crossing breakpoint `m` changes one adjacent `01` into `10`.
For the decimal value this adds

```text
9 * 10^(k - 1 - m)       for m < k,
1                        for m = k.
```

Thus the required sum can be obtained from the prefix sums of those increments
in breakpoint order.

For a Fibonacci denominator `q`, that breakpoint order is a modular
progression.  For a general size `n`, choose the next Fibonacci denominator
and rewrite the order recursively: the larger of the two previous Fibonacci
numbers gives the base order, and each selected base label contributes one
extra shifted label immediately before or after it.  This reduces the size by
a factor of about `phi` at each step.

The solver carries only four modular summary values for a stream: output
length, total increment, sum of prefixes, and sum of squared prefixes.  A
small piecewise-constant "macro" describes how a base label expands during the
recursion, so no list of labels or factors is materialized.  The initial lower
mechanical word is the prefix of `0` followed by the usual Fibonacci word, and
is evaluated with the same Fibonacci block decomposition.  Finally, the generic
geometric increment for `m = k` is corrected to the true final increment `1`.
