# Dice Box Arrangement Count

This solution counts arrangements of identical labelled dice in an `x by y by z` box, where touching faces must have the same value and rotations of a die give its 24 possible orientations.

## Main techniques

1. **Model orientations as signed basis triples**

   Treat the six die faces as the six signed coordinate directions `±e1`, `±e2`, and `±e3`. A die orientation is then a positively oriented signed basis.

2. **Remove alternating neighbour signs**

   If two dice touch along one grid direction, the same die face label appears on both touching faces. In signed-basis notation, this makes the corresponding basis vector flip sign from one cell to the next along that direction.

   After multiplying by coordinate-parity signs, the local condition becomes independent of the cell position: three arrays of signed directions must form a positively oriented basis at every grid point.

3. **Reduce the 3D problem to repeated 2D factorizations**

   For each layer index, choose two sequences of signed basis vectors, say `A` and `B`. Their cross products define a matrix `C`:

   ```text
   C[i][j] = A[j] x B[i]
   ```

   The full 3D count is obtained by counting how many times the same `C` matrix can arise from different layer factorizations.

4. **Classify possible matrices by factorization count**

   Every valid `C` has either 2 or 4 factorizations. For dimensions `x` and `y`:

   ```text
   # matrices with 4 factorizations: 6 * 2^(x + y - 2)
   # matrices with 2 factorizations: 3 * 2^(x + y - 1) * (2^x + 2^y - 4)
   ```

   Therefore, summing over all possible `C` matrices for `z` layers gives the symmetric closed form:

   ```text
   f(x, y, z) = 3 * 2^(x + y + z - 1) * (2^x + 2^y + 2^z - 4)
   ```
