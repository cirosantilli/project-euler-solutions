# Project Euler 850 Solution - Fractions of Powers

<https://projecteuler.net/problem=850>:

* [850.py](850.py)

## Problem Summary

For odd $k$ define

$$
f_k(n)=\sum_{i=1}^{n}\left\{\frac{i^k}{n}\right\}.
$$

The task is to compute

$$
\left\lfloor\sum_{\substack{1\le k\le N\\k\text{ odd}}}\sum_{n=1}^N f_k(n)\right\rfloor
\pmod {977676779}
$$

for $N=33557799775533$.

## Main Reductions

For odd $k$,

$$
(n-i)^k\equiv -i^k\pmod n.
$$

Thus the terms for $i$ and $n-i$ normally contribute fractional parts summing to $1$. The exception is when $n\mid i^k$, where both fractional parts are $0$. If

$$
m_k(n)=\prod_{p^e\parallel n}p^{\lceil e/k\rceil},
$$

then $n\mid i^k$ exactly when $m_k(n)\mid i$, giving $n/m_k(n)$ such values of $i$ in $1,\ldots,n$. Therefore

$$
2f_k(n)=n-\frac{n}{m_k(n)}.
$$

The code works modulo $2M$, where $M=977676779$, and divides by $2$ only at the end. This preserves whether the true value is integral or half-integral.

## Divisor Profile Formula

Let

$$
H(N)=\sum_{\substack{1\le k\le N\\k\text{ odd}}}\sum_{n=1}^N \frac{n}{m_k(n)}.
$$

The $k=1$ term is simply $N$. For odd $k\ge3$, define

$$
\rho_k(d)=\prod_{p^a\parallel d}p^{\lceil a/(k-1)\rceil}.
$$

Then

$$
\frac{n}{m_k(n)}=\sum_{\substack{d\ge1\\d\rho_k(d)\mid n}}\varphi(d).
$$

This identity can be checked prime by prime. For $n=p^E$, the allowed exponents $a$ in $d=p^a$ are those with

$$
a+\left\lceil\frac{a}{k-1}\right\rceil\le E,
$$

whose largest value is $E-\lceil E/k\rceil$. The totient sum over $p^0,\ldots,p^{E-\lceil E/k\rceil}$ is exactly $p^{E-\lceil E/k\rceil}$.

After summing over $n\le N$, the remaining quantity is

$$
H(N)=N+\sum_{\substack{k=3\\k\text{ odd}}}^N
\sum_{d\ge1}\varphi(d)\left\lfloor\frac{N}{d\rho_k(d)}\right\rfloor.
$$

Only profiles with $d\operatorname{rad}(d)\le N$ can contribute, since $\rho_k(d)\ge\operatorname{rad}(d)$ for $k\ge3$.

## How the Code Works

The solver sieves primes up to $\sqrt N$, then recursively enumerates all divisor profiles

$$
d=\prod p^a
$$

with $d\operatorname{rad}(d)\le N$. During recursion it keeps:

* $d$,
* $\operatorname{rad}(d)$,
* $\varphi(d)$ modulo $2M$,
* the largest exponent in the profile,
* the current list of $(p,a)$ pairs.

For a fixed profile, once odd $k$ is larger than every exponent $a$, all ceilings in $\rho_k(d)$ equal $1$, so $\rho_k(d)=\operatorname{rad}(d)$. The code sums that long tail as one block:

$$
\#\{k\text{ odd in the tail}\}\left\lfloor\frac{N}{d\operatorname{rad}(d)}\right\rfloor.
$$

Only the small odd $k$ before that tail are evaluated one by one. There are at most about $\log_2 N$ relevant exponent thresholds, so this is a short loop for each divisor profile.

Finally,

$$
2S(N)=\#\{k\le N:k\text{ odd}\}\sum_{n=1}^N n-H(N),
$$

computed modulo $2M$, and the printed answer is $\lfloor S(N)\rfloor\bmod M$.

## Checks

The solver asserts the statement examples in doubled form:

$$
2f_5(10)=9,\qquad 2f_7(1234)=1233,
$$

and also verifies

$$
2S(10)=201,\qquad 2S(1000)=247375608.
$$
